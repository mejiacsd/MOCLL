Microplastics and Marine Litter Policy and Youth

The UN MGCY presents an array of essential key points of actions to be undertaken by governmental and non-governmental actors. To sum up these key commitments further elaborated in our full position paper, we, as young people from all parts of the globe, request the government to improve their strategies to tackle marine litter and microplastics by:

  1.  Monitoring and Reporting

  2. Plastic Pollution Prevention & Promotion of a Circular Economy

  3. Sound & Circular Waste Management

Firstly, by ensuring a proper monitoring and reporting status of our marine and coastal environment as well as the magnitude of the use of plastic because we cannot combat what we donâ€™t know. 

Secondly, by setting up policies to prevent plastic pollution and promote circular economy. This can be done in multitude of ways e.g. by setting up reduction targets, and Promoting the reuse, recycling and recovery of materials. 

Lastly, we stress upon the importance of sound and circular waste management by ensuring sound disposal management of waste, particularly plastic waste. We condemn the practice of incineration since burning plastic products are releasing harmful chemicals in the air, polluting the environment and impacting the health.
